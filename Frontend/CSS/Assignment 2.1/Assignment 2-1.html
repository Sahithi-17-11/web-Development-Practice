MONALISA

Artist

Leonardo da Vinci (1452-1519), a polymath of the Italian Renaissance, is the artist behind the painting. He is considered one of the greatest painters of all time.
The Mona Lisa is housed at the Louvre Museum in Paris, France. It has been on display there since 1797 and attracts millions of visitors annually

Artistic Features

Sfumato: One of da Vinci's signature techniques, sfumato, is used in the Mona Lisa to create a soft, blurry effect around her face, especially her smile. This allows for a lifelike and mysterious aura, which has contributed to the fascination with the painting's expression.
Use of Light and Shadow: Da Vinci expertly used chiaroscuro (the contrast between light and dark) to give depth and volume to the figure of Lisa.
The Smile: The subject's smile is one of the most talked-about features. It appears to change depending on the viewer's perspective, sometimes appearing as a smile and other times as a more neutral expression. This effect is also attributed to sfumato.

Cost

The Mona Lisa is considered priceless, meaning it has no set market value. While it's nearly impossible to estimate an exact price, its worth is immeasurable in cultural, historical, and artistic terms.
In 1962, the Mona Lisa was insured for $100 million (which would be around $870 million today, adjusted for inflation). This was the highest insurance value ever placed on a painting at that time.
